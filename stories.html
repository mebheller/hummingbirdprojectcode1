A Legend of Native Lore



As a native of the Western Hemisphere, the hummingbird is a common character in the legends and myths of Native American peoples. In many native tribes, the hummingbird is associated with stories about love. The Mayans believed that the sun once adopted the disguise of a hummingbird in order to court a beautiful woman, who turned out to actually be the moon in disguise.

A Rainmaker



In many Native American legends, hummingbirds intervene with the gods to bring rain. Because of this, people from tribes such as the Hopi often paint hummingbirds on water jars. A Symbol of Renewal



For this reason the hummingbird is often seen as a symbol of regeneration. The Mojave people believed that in primordial times, people lived in an underground world of darkness, so they dispatched a hummingbird aboveground to find light for them; upon his return he led them through a secret path to live in the sunlight forever. The hummingbird is the creature that opens our hearts to love, beauty, possibility, vitality and the sweetness of life. Though small, the hummingbird is bright and full of power.
